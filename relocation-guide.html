The Facts

Cost of Living, Schools & Commute

Cost of Living

  • ~12% below the national average
  • No state income tax on Social Security
  • Median home price ~$420K (vs. $800K+ in DC/NYC)
  • Property taxes among the lowest in the Southeast
  • Utilities, groceries, and healthcare all competitive

Schools

  • Wake County Public School System — one of NC's best
  • Magnet school program with specialized tracks
  • Strong private school options (Cardinal Gibbons, Ravenscroft, etc.)
  • Proximity to NC State, Duke, UNC-Chapel Hill
  • Butch can match you to neighborhoods with your preferred schools

Commute & Access

  • I-540 outer loop connects most major suburbs
  • RDU International Airport — 20 min from most of Wake County
  • GoRaleigh bus system + GoTriangle regional transit
  • Remote work has reduced commute pressure significantly
  • Major employers: SAS, Lenovo, Red Hat, Wake Med, Duke Energy

Common Questions

Relocation FAQ

Do I need to visit Raleigh before making an offer?
Not necessarily. Many of Butch's out-of-state clients have bought successfully using video tours, neighborhood walkthroughs, and a single visit timed to the closing. That said, if it's feasible, a scouting trip — even a weekend — is always valuable.
How competitive is the Raleigh market for buyers?
The Triangle remains a seller's market in most price ranges, especially under $600K. Well-priced homes in popular neighborhoods often receive multiple offers within days. Having Butch in your corner means faster alerts, stronger offer strategies, and credibility with listing agents who know his name.
How does the school assignment process work in Wake County?
Wake County uses a modified neighborhood-based assignment system with magnet program options. Boundaries are reviewed periodically. Butch will pull the current school assignments for any home you're considering and flag any upcoming boundary changes.
What's the difference between Raleigh, Cary, and the other Triangle cities?
Raleigh is the capital city — urban core, government, and tech. Cary is planned, diverse, and suburban. Wake Forest is small-town with rapid growth. Apex and Holly Springs are family-friendly and frequently top "best places to live" lists. Durham has a revitalized arts scene. Chapel Hill is university-driven and progressive. Butch will match your lifestyle to the right fit.
Is there an income tax in North Carolina?
Yes — NC has a flat state income tax rate of 4.5% (as of 2025, on a declining path set by legislature). This is lower than most coastal states and much lower than states like California or New York. Social Security income is exempt from NC state tax.
