The Full Story
Butch Dyer
REALTOR®  ·  Relocation & New Construction Specialist

I got my real estate license in 1984 — the same year Raleigh was beginning to feel the first stirrings of the growth that would transform it into the city it is today. I didn't know then that I'd spend the next four decades watching it happen, neighborhood by neighborhood, family by family.

Over the years, I've helped everyone from military families on tight timelines to tech executives relocating their entire lives from California. I've worked with first-time buyers who had more questions than savings, and with long-time homeowners selling a home they'd raised three generations in. Every transaction is different. That's what keeps this work meaningful.

My specialty in relocation grew naturally out of the reality that Raleigh attracts people from everywhere. When someone is making a decision this significant from 1,000 miles away, they need more than an agent — they need an honest guide. I'm proud to be that for my clients.

On the new construction side, I've built relationships with virtually every major builder in the Triangle over decades. That means early access to communities, frank conversations about builder quality, and the ability to advocate for my clients in ways the builder's sales team simply can't.

Community leadership: Butch serves as an active HOA board member for his neighborhood, bringing that same organizational insight and community investment to his clients — helping them understand what questions to ask about any HOA they're considering joining.

How I Work

My Philosophy

Listen First

Before I show you a single home, I want to understand who you are, what matters to you, and what success looks like. Your priorities drive the process — not mine.

Honest Advice

If a home is priced too high, I'll tell you. If a neighborhood has issues I know about, I'll tell you. If your asking price is leaving money on the table, I'll tell you. Every time.

Long-Term Relationships

Most of my business comes from past clients and their families. That's not an accident — it's the result of treating every client like they're the only client. I'm still in touch with families from my very first years in the business.
