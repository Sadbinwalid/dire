Good to Know

Mortgage Basics

Common questions Butch hears from buyers at every stage.

What is PMI and when is it required?
PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required by most lenders when your down payment is less than 20% of the purchase price. It typically adds 0.5%–1.5% of the loan amount annually to your payment. Once you reach 20% equity, you can usually request removal. This calculator does not include PMI — factor it in separately if your down payment is below 20%.
Is a 15-year or 30-year mortgage better?
A 15-year mortgage has a higher monthly payment but much less total interest paid — you build equity faster and own your home outright sooner. A 30-year mortgage has a lower monthly payment, giving you more cash-flow flexibility each month. Which is better depends on your income stability, other financial goals, and risk tolerance. Try both in the calculator above to see the monthly difference for your specific situation.
How do property taxes work in Wake County, NC?
Wake County taxes are based on the assessed value of your property multiplied by the combined county and municipal rate. In most Raleigh-area municipalities, the combined rate runs roughly 0.95%–1.15% annually. The 1.05% default in this calculator is a reasonable midpoint. Your actual rate depends on which municipality your property is in — Raleigh, Cary, Wake Forest, and Apex each have slightly different rates.
What else will I pay beyond PITI?
Beyond principal, interest, taxes, and insurance, you may also have HOA dues (common in planned communities), flood insurance if you're in a designated flood zone, and possibly an umbrella policy. New construction communities often have higher HOA fees that cover common-area maintenance. Butch will always flag HOA amounts upfront when showing properties — they're a real part of your monthly cost.
Should I get pre-approved before I start looking?
Yes — almost always. A pre-approval letter shows sellers you're a serious buyer and gives you a clear budget before you fall in love with a home you can't afford. It also locks in a rate range and surfaces any credit issues early, while there's still time to fix them. Butch works with lenders who can move fast and treat clients right. Give him a call and he can make an introduction.
